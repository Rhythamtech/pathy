## Weekly Plan

### Week 1: Software Setup & Video Editing Fundamentals

**Goal:** Understand the video editing interface, import media, and perform basic cuts and trims.

**Primary course focus:** Video editing fundamentals, Tools and software for editing

**One YouTube resource:** [How to edit video on Instagram 2026 | How to edit video on Instagram | Reels editing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=isDy5sjX61Q) by Technical Saif

**Why this resource:** This video directly addresses the user's need for Instagram Reels editing with recent 2026 content, providing a beginner-friendly tutorial that complements the Primal Video course by focusing specifically on Instagram's native editing tools and techniques for creating client-ready reels.

**Practical work:** Install a professional editing software (e.g., Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, or DaVinci Resolve). Import sample footage (e.g., from your phone's camera roll). Create a new project, arrange clips on the timeline, and perform basic cuts to remove unwanted sections, resulting in a 30-60 second rough cut.

**Done when:** Successfully install and open the chosen editing software. Create a new project and sequence. Import at least 5 different video clips. Perform at least 10 accurate cuts and trims on the timeline to create a single continuous rough cut of 30-60 seconds without technical errors.

### Week 2: Essential Editing Tools & Polishing the Cut

**Primary course focus:** Video editing fundamentals, Workflow optimization

**Why this resource:** This video directly complements the primary course by providing a focused, practical tutorial on editing tools and polishing techniques for Instagram Reels. It's from the same trusted creator (Justin Brown - Primal Video), ensuring consistency in teaching style and credibility. The video is recent (2024) and addresses the specific beginner-to-client editing workflow, aligning with the user's target outcome and weekly learning pace. **Practical work:** Take the rough cut from Week 1. Use the razor tool to make precise splits. Apply ripple edits to remove clips without leaving gaps. Use the rate stretch tool to slightly adjust the speed of a clip to fit the timing. Add at least two simple transitions (like cross dissolve) between clips.

**Done when:** The rough cut is refined into a clean sequence. All clips are tightly edited with no unintended gaps. Transitions are applied correctly. The total duration is optimized for pacing (e.g., under 60 seconds).
